The road to learning physics is full of pitstops and bad diction. Here’s some literature to help on the way.

Mechanics Link to heading
  • D. Morin’s Mechanics
  • H. Goldstein’s Classical Mechanics
Quantum Mechanics Link to heading
  • D. Griffiths’ Introduction to Quantum Mechanics
  • R. Shankar’s Principles of Quantum Mechanics
  • S. Weinberg’s Lectures on Quantum Mechanics
  • R. Littlejohn’s [Lecture Notes]
  • Cohen-Tannoudji’s Quantum Mechanics
  • Zettili’s Quantum Mechanics
Electromagnetism Link to heading
  • D. Griffiths’ Introduction to Electrodynamics
  • J. Jacksons’ Classical Electrodynamics
Statistical Mechanics Link to heading
  • C. Kittel’s Elementary Statistical Physics
  • D. Tong’s [Lecture Notes on Statistical Physics]
  • M. Kardar’s Statistical Mechanics of Particles [Online Course]
  • M. Kardar’s Statistical Mechanics of Fields [Online Course]
Quantum Field Theory Link to heading
  • S. Blundell and T. Lancaster’s QFT for the Gifted Amateur
  • D. Tong’s [Lecture Notes on QFT]
  • M. Peskin and D. Schroeder’s Introduction to Quantum Field Theory
  • D. Tong’s [Lecture Notes on Gauge Theory]
  • S. Weinberg’s Quantum Theory of Fields
Condensed Matter Link to heading
  • C. Kittel’s Solid State Physics
  • N. Ashcroft and N. Mermin Solid State Physics
  • D. Khomskii’s Quantum Theory of Solids
  • A. Altland and B. Simon’s Condensed Matter Field Theory
  • E. Fradkin’s Condensed Matter Field Theory
