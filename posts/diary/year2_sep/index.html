Academically Link to heading

This month, I spent my time doing a rotation with Mike Zaletel. I looked into Density Matrix Renormalization Group (DMRG) method and topological order in the fractional quantum hall effect. I started to see how computationally nice one dimensional models are, how computationally hard two dimensional models are, and how hard three dimensions is. It fills me with a little sadness to know how hard it is to model three dimensional models. Density functional theory (DFT) seems to be our best bet and even then it is hard to reliably predict using DFT. In fact, even if DFT is wrong its hard to diagnose what happened and most just shrug and say you used the wrong pseudopotential…not very helpful :(

I attended a really interesting talk on finding an analytical formula for the hall number by Assa Auerbach.
